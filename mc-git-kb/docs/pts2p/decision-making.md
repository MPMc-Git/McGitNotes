| Framework | Elements |
| :--- | :--- |
| Chesterton's Fence | <small>- Don't tear down a process until you know why it exists.<br>- Prevent well-meaning but harmful change.</small> |
| Gall's Law | <small>- Complex systems that work start out simple.<br>- Build small, prove it, then scale.</small> |
| Goodhart's Law | <small>- When a metric becomes a target, it stops being a good metric.<br>- Vital reminder for any KPI or bonus system.</small> |
| Hanlon's Razor | <small>- Don't assume malice when confusion, bad info, or incentives explain the behavior.<br>- Useful for defusing politics and blam when stakes are high.</small> |
| Hickman's Dictum | <small>- More than one cause can be true at once.<br>- Helps when messy problems resist a single, tidy answer.</small> |
| Hofstadter's Law | <small>- Everything takes longer than you expect, even when you account for this fact.<br>- A humble check on any plan.</small> |
| Occam's Razor | <small>- Start with the simplest explanation that fits.<br>Complexity is often a symptom, not the solution.</small> |
| Parkinson's Law | <small>- Work expands to fill the time available.<br>- Shorter deadlines often force focus, not failure.</small> |
| Peter Principle | <small>- People rise to their level of incompetence.<br>- A nudge to test for future fit, not just past performance.</small> |
| Shirky Principle | <small>- Institutions tend to preserve the problem they were created to solve.<br>- Explains why some systems resist even smart change.</small> |
| Sturgeon's Law | <small>- Roughly 90% of everything is mediocre.<br>- Assume most ideas are average; hunt for the rare standout.</small> |
| Sutton's Law | <small>- Go where the money is.<br>- Attack the obvious, high-leverage issue before chasing exotic problems.</small> |
