## Do's and Dont's of DIFFICULT Conversations
**#BrillianceBrief.com**
**#JustinWright**

1. **Lead with Empathy**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Start by acknowleding their feelings and showing you care
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Dive straight into criticism or blame
1. **Stay Calm, Not Combaite**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Keep your tone steady and take a breath before responding
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Let frustration or adrenaline take over
1. **Prepare, but Stay Flexible**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Outline key points and possible solutions ahead of time
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Stick to a rigid script that ignores their perspective
1. **Ask to Understand**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Use open-ended questions to get their view
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Ask loaded questions that push them into a defensive corner
1. **Really Listen**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Give them your full attention - eye contact, nodding, mirroring what they say
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Check your phone, zone out, or just wait for your turn to speak
1. **Own Your Emotions**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Acknowledge how you feel, but keep it in check
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Play the victim or try to guilt them to your side
1. **Respect, Don't Diminish**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Validate their perspective, even if you don't agree
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Dismiss their concerns or talk down to them
1. **Be Clear & Direct**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Use real examples when addressing issues
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Generalize or exaggerate
1. **Focus on Solutions**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Work together to find a way forward
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Dump the problem on them without suggesting a fix
1. **Set Boundaries**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Be clear about what you can and can't commit to and why
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Say yes out of guilt or overpromise what you can't deliver
1. **Give Real Appreciation**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Acknowledge their efforts before suggesting changes
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Use fake praise just to soften criticism
1. **Apologize when Needed**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Own your mistakes and explain how you'll fix them
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Give half-heared apologies ("sorry you felt that way")
1. **End with a Clear Next Step**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Agree on actions and follow-ups before wrapping up
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Leave things vague and unresolved
1. **Reflect and Improve**
  <br>:white_check_mark:**Do**: Take a moment to review what work and what didn't
  <br>:x:**Don't**: Assume it's over and repeat the same mistakes next time
